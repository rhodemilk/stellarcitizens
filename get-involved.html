Get Involved

Petition

Petitions may be submitted online, in written letter form, or via email. It's also crucial to remember that the precise procedure for petitioning the government can differ depending on the problem or issue, therefore it might be good to seek advice from a legal or advocacy group.

Determine which particular government department or official is in charge of handling your problem or concern.

Clearly express your request or concern in writing, and gather any documentation or other proof you feel is necessary.

Send your petition to the proper government agency or official, adhering to any necessary instructions or formalities.

Contact the organization or representative again to check on the status of your petition and make sure it is being given the right attention.

Vote

The voting process depends on state specific rules but generally:

Register to vote: First register to vote with your local election office. This entails completing a registration form and presenting identification and residence documentation. While some states allow online voter registration, others only accept paper applications.

Receive your ballot: Depending on your specific state's voting laws, you will either get a ballot in the mail or at a polling site before election day. You may also be able to cast a ballot early, in person, or by mail.

Cast your vote: On Election Day or during early voting, you will visit a polling place and cast your vote. Your voting card will have your specific voting station if you chose to go on election day. Check out the ballot on your district’s website to be able to make the most educated voting choices.

Verify: Most states have a system in place where you can confirm your vote was received by inputting your voting card information.
